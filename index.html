Gabriel Brock

Political Scientist and Technologist
Washington, DC

I am a political scientist based in Washington, DC. My research focuses on public policy, big data, and bias and fairness. I use R-based computational statistics to design solutions for inequities in policing, education, and technology. I hold a Bachelor of Arts in Government, African-American Studies, and Computer Science from Harvard University with a focus in Data Science. At Harvard, I have served as a Teaching Fellow in Statistics and Data Research Specialist in the Department of Music, and I am currently the Kwame Anthony Appiah Prize Fellow for my work on disparity in stop-and-frisk policing.

Research

Selection of my academic research; published work, curricular projects, and conference papers.
  • G. Brock, Did Floyd Fix It?: Statistical Analysis of Racial Disparities in New York City's Stop-and-Frisk Program, 2026. Senior Honors Thesis. Advised by Francesca Arruda de Amaral. Completed a one-hundred-page thesis including in-depth study policing case law and econometric analysis of racial disparities in dataset of over 1.3 million unique stop records. Summa Cum Laude minus.
  • G. Brock, The Role of School Quality on Upward Mobility in Houston, TX, 2026. Applied multivariate regression to assess whether access to higher-performing schools predicts upward mobility for low-income students, cleaning and analyzing state accountability data, Census records, and outcome data from the Opportunity Atlas.
  • G. Brock, Urban Development and Voting Patterns in Harris County, TX, 2026. Examined the relationship between residential development, neighborhood change, and electoral outcomes in Harris County using precinct-level voting data and ACS census-tract estimates, identifying how urban growth patterns correlate with shifts in civic participation. Used geospatial analysis and developed original cross-walking method to transpose tract-level estimates to voter precinct-level.
  • G. Brock, Majority-Black Congressional Districts and Efficacy of Black Representation: A Text Analysis, 2024. Analyzed vote records and text analysis from Congressional district descriptions to identify majority-Black districts and examined voting records to assess representation efficacy.
  • G. Brock, Impact of Urban Development on Charter School Admissions Equity in St. Louis, MO, 2024. Supervised by Dr. Katherine Merseth. Studied how urban development and gentrification affects charter school admissions for students in St. Louis by combining residential activity data from the Census with school enrollment records. Research informed policy recommendations on how to combat displacement of low-income students from city school district. Presented at the 2023 Harvard Business School Social Enterprise Conference.
  • G. Brock, The Role of Place on Police Shootings, 2024. Investigated the relationship between police residence and fatal police shootings using a data science approach. It aims to discern patterns, trends, and potential biases associated with geographical proximity of police officers to the communities they police from 2015 to 2023.
  • G. Brock, Watching the Watchers: A Crowdsourced Approach to Protecting the Right to Assemble in the Age of Digital Surveillance, 2023. Developed a crowdsourced platform to monitor and document facial recognition NYPD surveillance of peaceful protests.
  • G. Brock, J. Nair, Can AI Pass for News?: Human Inability to Classify AI Images and Media Misinformation, 2023.
    • Conducted an experiment testing the capability of natural language programs (NLPs) to generate images for social media-focused news content. We then measured how well humans classify images in simulated interaction.
